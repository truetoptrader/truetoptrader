The only number
that matters is R

Most traders aren't profitable. They're good at hiding risk. We don't show profit. We show how it was made.

Why R 01

Profit percentages can be inflated by leverage, martingale, averaging into losses. R — the ratio of reward to risk — cannot. A trader with +50R over 100 trades is either consistently good or consistently lucky. The sample size reveals which.

Why entries are locked 02

Every entry price is set by the system at the exact millisecond of publication. No manual input. No backdating. No "I called it in my head." If it's not in the system, it doesn't count.

Why trades are permanent 03

Once published, a trade cannot be deleted or edited. Losses stay on the record. Cherry-picking exits is the oldest trick in signal selling. It doesn't work here.

For traders 04

SIGNAL is the most honest way to build a public track record. No marketing. No screenshots. Just numbers. If you're good — followers will find you.

For followers 05

Every trader you see here has a verifiable history. You can see every trade they ever published. Before you follow anyone's signals, you now have data. Use it.

Rules

Publishing a trade

  • Choose instrument, direction, Stop Loss and Take Profit.
  • Entry is set by the system at publish time — no manual entry.
  • Once published, trades cannot be edited or deleted.

Trade resolution

  • TP hit — price reaches Take Profit → result in R recorded.
  • SL hit — price reaches Stop Loss → result = −1R.
  • Expired — open 48+ hours → result = 0R.

R calculation

Risk = distance to Stop Loss = 1R. Reward is proportional. TP twice as far as SL = +2R result.

Anti-abuse

  • Maximum 3 active trades at once.
  • Minimum stop loss enforced per instrument.
  • SL must be on the correct side of entry.

Rankings

Ranked by cumulative R from all closed trades. Consistency grade (A+ to C) factors in winrate and average R.
