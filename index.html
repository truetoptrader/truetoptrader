Rules

How it works

Signal is a public trade rating platform. You publish a trade signal and the system tracks whether it hits TP, SL, or expires — automatically.

Publishing a trade

  • Choose instrument, direction (BUY/SELL), Stop Loss and Take Profit.
  • Entry price is automatically set by the system at the exact moment you publish — no manual entry allowed.
  • Once published, a trade cannot be edited or deleted.

Trade resolution

  • TP hit: price reaches your Take Profit → result calculated in R.
  • SL hit: price reaches your Stop Loss → result = −1R.
  • Expired: trade open for more than 48 hours → result = 0R (neutral).

R calculation

Risk to your Stop Loss = 1R. Reward is proportional: if TP is twice as far as SL from entry, result = +2R.

Anti-abuse rules

  • Maximum 3 active trades at one time.
  • Minimum stop loss size enforced per instrument (0.05% for majors, up to 0.15% for metals).
  • Trades with SL on the wrong side of entry are rejected.

Ranking

Traders are ranked by total cumulative R from all closed trades. Consistency grade (A+/A/B+/B/C+/C) factors in winrate and average R per trade.
